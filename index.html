What is Chessboxing?

Chessboxing is a hybrid sport, where players compete in rotating rounds of both chess and boxing. The skills of both are crucial to winning, however only one victory from either from the game of chess of the boxing match is required to name a winner.

Rules of Chessboxing:

  • Players must not deliberately waste time when playing the chess discipline of the game. If the referees deem that they are then a 10 second penalty will be placed.
  • Players can win from either the chess or boxing rounds
  • 6 rounds of chess and 5 rounds of boxing will be completed unless the contest is stopped with a winner in a previous round.
How to Win:

To be declared as the winner of a chessboxing match, one of the following must occur:

  • Victory by knockout or technical knockout in boxing
  • Victory by checkmate in chess
  • Loss due to exceeding the chess game's time control
  • Loss by resignation in either chess or boxing
  • Victory due to disqualification of opponent by the referee in either discipline following multiple warnings
